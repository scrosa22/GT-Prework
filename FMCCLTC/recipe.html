Homemade gnocchi. Sound intimidating? Just take a deep breath because this recipe requires only a few ingredients and can be done with no fancy equipment! By the end of the day you will have all the skills and confidence of Michelin star chef Gordon Ramsay!

While making gnocchi from scratch sounds tricky, the actual process is pretty easy and well worth the effort. This recipe for homemade gnocchi is a family tradition, though making gnocchi can be done in several different ways.

Once you know how to make gnocchi, what sauce you choose to pair it with is up to you and your palate!

Note - no fancy tools are necessary to make this, however some specialized tools will certainly help! I have included a tools resource below.

Also, to choose the right type of potato. You need a potato that is starchy with minimum water content like a Russet potato.

Now that you have your potatoes, you are ready to get started! Follow along below;

Ingredients

  • 4 Potatoes
  • 1 Egg
  • 2 Cups Flour
  • Cornmeal
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt

    • *General rule of thumb* 1 medium-sized potato per serving or person. For every potato, use approximately 1/2 cup of flour

Tools

  • Potato Ricer
  • Peeler
  • Sieve
  • Measuring Cups
  • Bench Scraper
  • Baking Sheet
  • Large Pot
  • Colander
  • Gnocchi Board
  • Mixing Bowl

Homemade Gnocchi Instructions

  1. The first step is to prepare the workspace by cleaning the countertop. Once the workspace is clean begin by peeling the potatoes and discarding the peels in a compost or trash bin.

  2. Next step, fill a bowl with cold water to place peeled potatoes in. Let the potatoes soak. (This will help to remove excess starch.) Add the potatoes to a large pot of salted water and bring it to a boil. Cover with a lid and let boil for 20 - 25 min.

    The potatoes should be "Fork Tender", meaning a fork can be pressed into them without much force.

    Once tender, take the potatoes out of the water and allow them to sit in a colander to get rid of any excess water.

  3. On the countertop, scoop two cups of flour into the sieve and sift onto clean counter space. Then, using the measuring cup make a well in the pile of flour. Form the well large enough to fit the other ingredients.

  4. Next, crack an egg into a cup and set it aside. Using the potato ricer, rice the potatoes into the well.

    Then, pour the cracked egg into the well, and use a fork to briefly whisk the egg. Add a pinch of salt and a teaspoon of olive oil.

    Then using your bench scraper (or using your hands), gently combine all the ingredients together and knead to form a dough ball. Sifting additional flour as needed,
    *The SECRET to perfect gnocchi lies in this step! Be mindful of overmixing and overflouring it! Knead just enough for the dough to come together, about 10 minutes. It should have a loose, airy texture (once you poke the dough and see it bounce back.) The biggest culprit to chewy homemade gnocchi is too much flour, when adding flour while making the dough the exact measurements will vary based on the actual size of the potato, egg, and the amount of humidity. Add it gradually as needed.

  5. Once the dough ball is complete, lightly sprinkle it with salt.

    Then put the dough in a covered bowl. Let it sit for roughly 30 minutes.

  6. Dust the countertop with flour and put the dough on floured surface.

    Then using a bench scraper cut the ball into 4 pieces.

    Take one piece, cover the remaining 3, and roll it into a log about 1" thick.

    Repeat process with the remaining dough pieces.

  7. Dust the baking sheets with corn meal and toss cut pieces in corn meal to prevent sticking.

    Using a gnocchi board (the back of a fork will do) roll gnocchi on board or fork to create grooves. While creating the grooves for gnocchi is not needed it makes perfect little pockets for the sauce to sit in!

    Repeat until all the gnocchi is rolled.

  8. Gently shake away any excess flour from your gnocchi.

    Add the gnocchi to a pot of boiling water and cook until the gnocchi float to the top

    When they rise to the top, remove with a slotted spoon and drain well, about 2 to 4 minutes.

  • Heat up your favorite sauce to pair with the fresh gnocchi! A few personal favorites; a roasted pomodoro, a fresh pesto, or even a creamy butter and sage sauce!

    Buon Appetito!

  • Additional Notes - Gnocchi freeze very well.

    Uncooked gnocchi can be frozen on the baking sheet, then transferred to a zip-top bag and frozen for up to 2 months.

    To cook them, just drop the frozen gnocchi directly in the boiling water without defrosting.
